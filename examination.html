ASSESSMENT SCHEME

  • There will be two terms in the Academic Session 2022-2023.
    • Term I : April 2022 - September 2022
    • Term II: October 2022 - March 2023

      • EVS - Diagrammatic Skills and Class Response
      • Notebook Work
        • Letter grades based on a **7 point scale will be awarded in each subject.
        • Criteria based Scholastic Skills in each subject (English, Hindi, Mathematics and EVS) will be assessed on a **7 point letter grade scale.
        • Co-Scholastic Skills in areas of Social & Emotional Development, Cleanliness and Discipline, Work Habits, Physical Education/ Sports, Dance, Music and Art will also be assessed on a 3 level Scale based on consistency of performance - (Always , Usually, Rarely)

      ACADEMIC SESSION : 2022-2023 CLASSES : III, IV & V

      ASSESSMENT SCHEME

      CLASSES III - V

      • There will be two terms in the Academic Session 2022-2023.
        • Term I : April 2022 - September 2022
        • Term II: October 2022 - March 2023
      • CORE SUBJECTS -: Each Term will have assessments in the form of Unit Assessments, Term Assessments and Internal Assessments in all core subjects i.e. English, Hindi, Mathematics and EVS. Details are as follows:

      TERMS I & II:

      Unit Assessments :

      • Class III - Each Term will have one round of Unit Assessments. Unit Assessment -1 will take place in Term I and Unit Assessment - 2 will be conducted in Term II. Each round of Unit Asssment will be of 25 marks in each of the 4 core subjects.
      • Classes IV & V - Each term will have two rounds of Unit Assessments . Unit Assessment– 1 & 2 will take place in Term I and Unit Assessment– 3 & 4 will be conducted in Term II. Each round of Unit Assessment with Objective and Subjective parts in each of the 4 core subjects will be as follows:
        • Class IV - 30 marks.
        • Class V - 40 marks

      Internal Assessment of 20 marksper term in each core subject from specific portions of the syllabi will include:

      • Project Work / Subject Enrichment Activity
      • Subject Specific Skills
        • English & Hindi - Speaking and Reading / Recitation Skills
        • Mathematics - Objective Assessment (Math Application Skills)
        • EVS - Objective Assessment ( Diagrammatic / Map skills )
      • Written Work & Class Response

      Term Assessments One Term Assessment per term for each of the 4 core subjects. These will be termed as Term I Assessment & Term II Assessment respectively in each term with Objective and Subjective parts as follows :

      • Class III - 50 marks ( Objective : 25 marks + Subjective : 25 marks )
      • Class IV - 50 marks ( Objective : 20 marks + Subjective : 30 marks )
      • Class V - 60 marks ( Objective : 20 marks + Subjective : 40 marks )

      Term wise weightage and distribution of marks in each core subject will be as follows :

      CLASS III

      Team Type of Assessment Total Marks Weightage
      Terms I / II Unit Assessment - 1 & 2 25 marks 10 marks
      Internal Assessment - 1 & 2 20 marks 10 marks
      Term Assessment I & II 50 marks 30 marks
      TOTAL TERM I MARKS ( A) = (10 marks + 10 marks + 30 marks) x 2 = 100 marks (100%) TOTAL TERM II MARKS( B) = (10 marks + 10 marks + 30 marks) x 2 = 100 marks (100%)
      FINAL ASSESSMENT = (50% of A + 50% of B ) = 50 marks + 50 marks = 100 marks (100%)

      CLASS IV

      Team Type of Assessment Total Marks Weightage
      Terms I / II Unit Assessment - 1 & 3 25 marks 10 marks

      Term Assessment I & II 50 marks 30 marks
      TOTAL TERM I MARKS (A) = 15 marks + 15 marks + 20 marks + 50 marks = 100 marks (100 %) TOTAL TERM II MARKS (B) = 15 marks + 15 marks + 20 marks + 50 marks = 100 marks (100 %)
      FINAL ASSESSMENT = (50% of A + 50% of B ) = 50 marks + 50 marks = 100 marks (100%)

      CLASS V

      Team Type of Assessment Total Marks Weightage
      Terms I / II Unit Assessment - 1 & 3 40 marks 10 marks
      Unit Assessment - 2 & 4 40 marks 20 marks
      Internal Assessment – 1 & 2 20 marks 20 marks
      Term Assessment I & II 60 marks 60 marks
      TOTAL TERM I MARKS (A) = 10 marks + 10 marks + 20 marks + 60 marks = 100 marks (100 %) TOTAL TERM II MARKS (B) = 10 marks + 10 marks + 20 marks + 60 marks = 100 marks (100 %)
      FINAL ASSESSMENT = (50% of A + 50% of B ) = 50 marks + 50 marks = 100 marks (100%)

      • Each Term will have one assessment of 30 marks each in Computer Science, Third Language and General Awareness.

      Please Note :

      1. Letter grades based on a 9 point scale will be awarded in each subject.
      2. Average of marks will be considered for the award of grades for the students absent in any of the Unit Assessments or Term Assessments NO RETESTS will be conducted for the same.
      3. In each term, syllabus tested in the Unit Assessments of all subjects will also be tested in the Term Assessments for classes IV & V.
      4. In class V, marks will be deducted for spelling errors in the Unit Assessments & Term Assessments (in EVS subject).
      5. Criteria based Scholastic Skills in each subject (English, Hindi, Mathematics and EVS) will be assessed on a 7 point letter grade scale.
      6. Co-Scholastic Skills in areas of Social & Emotional Development , Cleanliness & Discipline, Work Habits ,Physical Education/ Sports , Dance , Music and Art will also be assessed on a 3 level scale based on consistency of performance.

      CLASSES XI & XII

      Attendance requirement:

      All students are required to complete 75% of attendance to make him/her eligible to take the examination.

      Subjects offered:

      English Core, Maths, Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Computer Science, Economics, Business Studies, Accountancy, Physical Education, Psychology, Geography, Hindi, History, Commercial Arts, Sociology, Political Science, Legal Studies, Informatics Practices.

      Rules for Change of Subject:
      • Change of subject(s) in class XI may be allowed by the Head of the School but not later than 15 July of that academic session.
      • No candidate shall be permitted to change his subject of study after passing Class XI.
      • The candidate shall not be offered a subject in Class XII which he has not studied and passed in class XI.

      SCHEME OF EXAMINATIONS AND PASS CRITERIA

      Senior School Certificate Examination : XII
      1. The Board conducts examination in all subjects except General Studies, Work Experience, Physical and Health Education, which will be assessed internally by the school.
      2. Pattern, weightage and duration of the Board exams will be as per the latest guidelines issued by CBSE. In subjects requiring practical examination, there will be a theory paper and practical examinations as required in the syllabi.
      3. In Work experience, General Studies and Physical and Health Education, the School will maintain cumulative records of student's periodical achievements and progress during the year.
      4. In order to be declared as having passed the examination, a candidate shall obtain a grade higher than E (i.e. at least 33% marks) in all the five subjects of external examination in the main or at the end of the compartmental examination. The pass marks in each subject of external examination shall be 33%. In case of a subject involving practical work a candidate must obtain 33% marks in theory and 33% marks in practical separately in addition to 33% marks in aggregate in order to qualify in that subject.

      For further information, please refer to the CBSE website :www.cbse.nic.in

      ENCOURAGING EXCELLENCE THROUGH AWARDS

      • Scholar Badge :Scholar Badge is awarded to a student securing overall 80% and above. Subjects to be considered for the award of a Scholar Badge will include: For classes VI -VIII : English, Hindi, III Language, Maths, Science, Social Science & Computers For classes IX & X : English, II Language, Maths, Science & Social Science For class XI : All five subjects opted for, with minimum 50% in each subject.
      • Scholar Blazer:A Scholar Blazer is awarded to a student who earns a Scholar Badge for three consecutive years. (Class V onwards)
      • Scholar Blazer & Tie: A Scholar Blazer and Tie is awarded to a student who earns a Scholar Badge for six consecutive years. (Class V onwards)
      • Gold Medal: A Gold Medal is awarded to a student who earns a Scholar Badge for seven consecutive years (Class V onwards)
      • Perfect Attendance Certificate:It is awarded to students for maintaining 100% attendance throughout the academic year.
      • Certificate of Excellence:It is awarded to students of class XII for excellence in Research / Innovation/ Sports / Cultural activities
